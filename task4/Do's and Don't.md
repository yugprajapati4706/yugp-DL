# Guide: Responsible Social Media for College Students





|Category|The Do's|The Don'ts"|
|-|-|-|
|Privacy \& Security|Do audit your privacy settings regularly to ensure you know who can see your personal posts.|Don't post sensitive information like your home address, current GPS location, or student ID number.|
|Professionalism|Do use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your academic projects, certifications, and technical skills.|Don't post content that depicts illegal activities, excessive partying, or unprofessional behavior.|
|Communication|Do engage in healthy, respectful debates and "think before you post" when emotions are high.|Don't use your platform to harass, bully, or spread unverified rumors about peers or faculty.|
|Credibility|Do verify information from multiple reliable sources before sharing or "retweeting" news.|Don't share "clickbait" or misinformation that could damage your reputation as a rational thinker.|
|Self-Management|Do curate a feed that inspires you, following industry leaders in AI, Mathematics, or your specific field.|Don't let social media consumption interfere with your sleep, physical health, or academic deadlines|
