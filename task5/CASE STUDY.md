Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
UPI/Online Payment Fraud involves deceptive tactics used to trick individuals into authorizing fraudulent transactions via Unified Payments Interface (UPI) platforms. In an era of instant digital payments, this crime has become a prevalent threat to financial security.
The Anatomy of the Fraud
The crime typically unfolds in these steps:
1.	Deceptive Hook: A fraudster poses as a buyer for an item listed on a marketplace or as a customer care executive.
2.	The "Collect" Request: Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a UPI "collect" request to the victim’s mobile app, often disguised as a payment notification.
3.	The Manipulation: The victim is pressured or manipulated into clicking "Pay" and entering their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving funds.
4.	Transaction Execution: Once the PIN is entered, the victim’s bank account is debited, and the funds are transferred to the fraudster’s account.
Targets and Consequences
Who is targeted: While anyone using digital payments is at risk, those who are less familiar with digital financial literacy, such as the elderly or novice online sellers, are primary targets. Fraudsters often exploit the urgency and confusion surrounding digital transactions.
The Consequences: The immediate result is the unauthorized loss of money. Beyond the financial impact, victims often face significant distress and difficulty in recovering funds due to the instant, irreversible nature of UPI transfers. In some cases, once a victim is compromised, they may be subjected to further scams, leading to long-term financial insecurity and loss of trust in digital payment ecosystems.
